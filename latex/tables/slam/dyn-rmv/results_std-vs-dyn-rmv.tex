    Effect of the proposed offline dynamics removal mechanism

    distribution of the 2D occupancy grid maps generated by RicoSLAM across
    five datasets of 2D laser scan sequences:
    IILABS~3D~\cite{ribeiro:access:2025}, Avantgarde, Cappero,
    Flowbotic~\cite{flowbotic}, and KUKA.
    Two operating modes considered in this experiment:
    \emph{scan} (each keyframe represents a scan)
    and \emph{local} (each keyframe represents a local map).
    For each sequence and mode, two occupancy grid maps are generated at cell
    resolution $\SI{0.03}{\metre}$:
    the \emph{standard} variant rasterizes the retained scans only through the
    Bresenham~\cite{bresenham:ibm:1965,zingl:uas:2016} line algorithm,
    while the \emph{dyn-rmv} variant applies the offline dynamics removal
    mechanism discussed in \secref{sec:slam:dynamics:offline} before rasterization.
    Evaluation metrics:
    $N_{\mathrm{obs.}}$, the number of valid (observed) cells in each map;
    $\Delta N_{\mathrm{obs.}}$, the difference in $N_{\mathrm{obs.}}$ between
    the \emph{dyn-rmv} and the \emph{standard} variants; and the
    peak/middle mass partition
    ($m_{\mathrm{peak}}, m_{\mathrm{mid}}$) of the empirical occupancy
    distribution over the observed cells of each map, formulated in
    \eqref{eq:slam:results:dynamics:metrics:peak-vs-middle}, for two threshold
    values $\tau \in \{0.05, 0.10\}$. By construction,
    $m_{\mathrm{peak}}(\tau)+m_{\mathrm{mid}}(\tau)=1$ for each $\tau$,
    so a sharper occupancy distribution corresponds to a larger
    $m_{\mathrm{peak}}$ and a smaller $m_{\mathrm{mid}}$.
    Bold entries indicate that all results from the \emph{dyn-rmv} variant
    produce a sharper occupancy distribution than the \emph{standard} variant
    for the same sequence, mode, and threshold.%
